LIFE SKILLS - DEVELOPING SOCIAL ENTREPRENEURS (Teachers: Evi Parissi – Panayiota Arvaniti)
Schools play a key role not only in imparting knowledge but also in developing the necessary life skills which promote and sustain young people’s emotional and social health. Life skills are a set of basic skills that enable us to effectively manage the challenges and questions we face in our daily lives. They include confidence, assertiveness, decision-making, and the ability to stay safe and healthy.
For the past four years the British Council has been organising and delivering their Life Skills – Developing Social Entrepreneurs programme in schools and our 2nd-year lyceum students embarked on the project of visualizing and creating virtual social enterprises which address social and environmental issues. The project also helped students to develop their language skills by involving them in communicative activities in which they had to answer complex questions, explain choices through structured reasoning, actively listen to others’ contributions and present their ideas to their classmates. As part of the programme the students also attended an inspiring speech by Mr Cliff Parry, Academic Manager of the British Council in Greece who guided them towards discovering the social entrepreneur within themselves. Here is a brief
description of the three social enterprises our students envisioned: The Green Grin Machine – Students: Spyridoula Vrachnou, Marianina Goutzouri, Maria
Katsarou, Katerina Bare, Elisavet Tomai, Stamatis Holevas
The ‘Green Grin Machine’ is a mobile canteen van which will travel around Greece and offer healthy meal choices at affordable prices. The menu will include a wide range of three-course meals (Mediterranean, vegetarian, vegan, etc.) in environmentally friendly packaging. The main ingredients will be sourced from Greek producers and all organic waste will be composted. The hectic pace of life has had an impact on people’s food choices with junk food being prevalent since it is widely available and relatively cheap. The Green Grin Machine will offer real, healthy food-to-go even in areas where affordable, good quality food is scarce while also protecting the environment. (Logo designed by Elisavet Tomai)
Give a hand – Students: Matina Efthymiou, Anastasia Kourti, Vassilis Bellos, Dimitris Ikonomidis, Maria Fellouzi, Christina Psilia
Give a hand markets medical equipment and prosthetic implants while also offering job opportunities to physically disabled people. Part of the profits are used to provide prosthetic implants for free to people who need them but cannot afford them or have no social security. Through campaigning and hiring disabled people the company also aims at raising awareness about the needs of people with disabilities. (Logo designed by Matina Efthymiou)
HOMESICK SEI – Students: Irini Kostopoulou, Sam Bouzaounde, Korina Nikolakou, Panagiotis Nikolopoulos, Spyros Tsiros, Yiannis Nikolaidis, Gerasimos Bahas, Mary Perera, Stefania Fragopoulou, Christos Simos
HOMESICK SEI (Social Enterprise Initiative) project is a social enterprise which will function as a sustainable tool to tackle the homelessness issue in the area of Piraeus. The business supports homeless people by earning income to cover their needs. It focuses on the provision of food, temporary accommodation and medical care. It collaborates with the local council, a network of professionals, from restaurant and bookshop owners to pharmacists and doctors who are active in the area. The company also relies on individual donations. What the students aimed at was combining the social objectives of a non-profit, human-centred organization with the goals of a proper money-making business which would actually operate in the business world.
